## Fire Risk Assessment for Restaurants

Restaurants, cafes, and takeaways present significant fire risks due to the combination of cooking equipment, open flames, hot oils, and public occupancy. Under the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005, a fire risk assessment must be carried out by the responsible person, with particular attention to kitchen areas where the majority of fires originate.

### Key Considerations

- Kitchen extraction fire risk and grease buildup
- Cooking oil and fat fire hazards
- Means of escape for dining areas and customer routes
- Fire suppression in kitchen areas

- Fire detection and warning systems
- Emergency lighting
- Fire fighting equipment
