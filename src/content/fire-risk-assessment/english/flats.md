## Fire Risk Assessment for Flats

Residential flats and apartment blocks require a fire risk assessment that addresses both individual dwelling safety and the fire safety of common areas shared by all residents. Under the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005, the responsible person for the building must ensure that adequate fire safety measures are in place throughout the communal areas and the building structure.

### Key Considerations

- Common area fire safety and maintenance
- Compartmentation between flats to prevent fire spread
- Escape route planning for multi-storey blocks
- Fire door integrity and self-closing devices

### What We Assess

- Means of escape routes and signage
- Fire detection and warning systems
- Emergency lighting
- Fire fighting equipment
- Fire safety management procedures
- Compartmentation between individual flats
