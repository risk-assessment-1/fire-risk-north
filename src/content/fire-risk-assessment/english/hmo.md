## Fire Risk Assessment for HMOs

Houses in Multiple Occupation (HMOs) carry a heightened fire risk due to the number of unrelated occupants sharing facilities and escape routes. Landlords and managing agents have a legal duty under the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 to ensure adequate fire safety measures are in place, and local authorities often impose additional licensing conditions requiring up-to-date fire risk assessments.

### Key Considerations

- Bedroom fire doors and escape windows
- Kitchen fire safety in shared facilities
- Shared area escape routes and lighting
- Tenant fire safety information and signage

### What We Assess

- Means of escape routes and signage
- Fire detection and warning systems
- Emergency lighting
- Fire fighting equipment
- Fire safety management procedures
- Room of origin fire containment
