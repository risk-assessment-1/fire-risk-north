## What is an Asbestos Survey?

An **asbestos survey** is a physical inspection of a building to find, record, and assess asbestos-containing materials (ACMs). A qualified surveyor examines the fabric of the property, takes samples of suspect materials, and sends them to a UKAS accredited laboratory for confirmed identification.

If your building was constructed before the year 2000, there is a strong chance it contains asbestos. The material was used in everything from ceiling tiles and floor adhesives to pipe insulation, boiler flues, and roofing sheets. It was not fully banned in the UK until 1999.

Under **Regulation 4 of the Control of Asbestos Regulations 2012**, anyone with responsibility for maintaining a non-domestic building has a legal duty to manage asbestos. That duty starts with knowing whether asbestos is present — and the only way to confirm that is through a survey.

### Three Types of Asbestos Survey

- **Management Survey** — The standard survey for buildings in normal use. Locates ACMs that could be disturbed during routine maintenance or day-to-day occupation. Low invasiveness, accessible areas only. This is the survey most building owners need first.

- **Refurbishment Survey** — Required before any building work begins. More intrusive than a management survey — the surveyor accesses areas behind walls, above ceilings, below floors, and inside service risers. Covers the specific areas where work is planned.

- **Demolition Survey** — Carried out before full or partial demolition. The most invasive type. The surveyor systematically inspects every part of the structure, including areas that require destructive access, to locate all asbestos in the building.

### What Our Asbestos Survey Includes

- Full site inspection by a UKAS accredited asbestos surveyor
- Bulk sampling of suspect materials and laboratory analysis (UKAS accredited lab)
- Asbestos register listing all identified and presumed ACMs
- Risk assessment matrix scoring each ACM by condition, accessibility, and likelihood of disturbance
- Management plan with prioritised actions (for management surveys)
- Photographic evidence of all sampled materials and ACM locations
- Written recommendations for remediation, encapsulation, or ongoing management

Lab results are returned within 48 to 72 hours. The full survey report is delivered as a digital document, ready to share with contractors, tenants, and your solicitor.

### Why Asbestos Surveys Matter

Asbestos exposure kills around **5,000 people every year** in the UK — more than road traffic accidents. The three main diseases are mesothelioma, lung cancer, and asbestosis. Diseases can take 15 to 50 years to develop after exposure, and there is no safe level of exposure to asbestos fibres.
