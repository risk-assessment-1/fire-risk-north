## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is a fire risk assessment?

A fire risk assessment is a systematic evaluation of a premises to identify fire hazards, assess the risk to occupants, and determine what fire safety measures are needed. It is a legal requirement under the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005.

### Who needs a fire risk assessment?

Every non-domestic premises in England and Wales must have a suitable and sufficient fire risk assessment. This includes offices, shops, hotels, restaurants, care homes, schools, warehouses, blocks of flats (common areas), and Houses in Multiple Occupation (HMOs).

### How often should a fire risk assessment be reviewed?

A fire risk assessment should be reviewed regularly — at least annually — and immediately after any significant changes to the premises, such as renovation, change of use, or after a fire incident.

The time depends on the size and complexity of the premises. A typical small-to-medium premises takes 2-4 hours to inspect. We deliver the written report within 24 hours of the inspection.

### What happens if I don't have a fire risk assessment?

Failure to comply with fire safety regulations can result in unlimited fines, prosecution, or even imprisonment. The local fire and rescue authority can issue enforcement notices or prohibit the use of premises.
